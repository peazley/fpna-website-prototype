Committees

Where the work happens.

FPNA's day-to-day work is done by volunteer committees. Each focuses on a specific area of neighborhood life and meets independently between general meetings. All committees are open to FPNA members.

Land Use

Reviews development proposals affecting the neighborhood and submits comment letters to the City of Portland and Multnomah County. Meets monthly.

Land Use page →

Communications

Writes the Updates newsletter, manages the website, and coordinates outreach. Owns FPNA's voice and channels.

Get in touch →

Wildfire & Emergency Preparedness

Coordinates defensible-space education, evacuation planning, and partnership with PF&R on neighborhood fire readiness.

Get in touch →

Forest Park Stewardship

Liaison between FPNA and the Forest Park Conservancy, the City Parks bureau, and trail volunteer programs.

Get in touch →

Power & Tree Management

Works with PGE on tree-trimming and outage reduction, building on years of advocacy that has reduced power issues for residents.
