Committees

Where the work happens.

FPNA's day-to-day work is done by volunteer committees. Each focuses on a specific area of neighborhood life and meets independently between general meetings. All committees are open to FPNA members.

Land Use

Reviews land use notices and applications, drafts comment letters, and may file appeals on behalf of FPNA. Per bylaws Article IX §6.

Land Use page →

Communications

Supports neighborhood engagement, event planning, the website, social media, and outreach. Per bylaws Article IX §1.

board@forestparkneighbors.org →

50th Anniversary Planning (ad-hoc)

Coordinates neighborhood volunteer engagement, grant writing, partner presence, and local business outreach for the August 22 celebration.

board@forestparkneighbors.org →

Other neighborhood concerns are real and ongoing but are not necessarily formal FPNA committees — broadband access, Firewise, Neighborhood Emergency Teams (NETs), Forest Park stewardship, and PGE tree-trimming coordination are examples. Residents working in those areas are welcome to coordinate through the board.

Volunteer with a committee.

Most committees are open to new volunteers and meet between general FPNA meetings. If you'd like to join one, send a note to the contact listed for that committee, or reach out to the board.
