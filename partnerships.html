Partnerships

The organizations we work with.

FPNA works alongside neighborhood, community, and environmental organizations across the Forest Park area and the broader Portland region.

Coalition affiliation

District Four Coalition.

FPNA is a member neighborhood association of the District Four Coalition (D4C), a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that holds the City of Portland's neighborhood services contract for District 4. D4C provides administrative, technical, and communications support to its member NAs.

districtfourcoalition.org →

From Neighbors West-Northwest to D4C

FPNA was previously a member of Neighbors West-Northwest (NWNW), one of Portland's historical neighborhood coalitions. As part of the City of Portland's transition to a district-based council, NWNW reorganized as the District Four Coalition. FPNA's bylaws still reference NWNW; updates are in progress.

Living with the land

Resources we curate, not duplicate.

FPNA points residents to the best existing resources rather than reinventing them. Wildlife coexistence, wildfire preparedness, and emergency planning all have strong regional partners.

Safety contacts

Three jurisdictions.

The FPNA boundary spans the City of Portland, unincorporated Multnomah County, and touches Washington County. Residents may have different emergency contacts depending on where they live.

Multnomah County Sheriff's Office · Washington County Sheriff's Office · Portland Police Bureau. For all emergencies, call 911. See the Contact page for non-emergency numbers.
