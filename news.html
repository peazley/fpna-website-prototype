City Code 3.96 and the ONI Standards.

The rules that govern how Portland's neighborhood associations operate are being rewritten. This affects FPNA and all 93 other recognized NAs. Informational only — D4C leads the coordinated response on behalf of its member associations.

What City Code 3.96 is

City Code Title 3.96 is the authorizing framework for Portland's neighborhood system. It defines the relationship between the city, the district coalitions, and the recognized NAs.

What the Standards do

The Standards (formally, the "Standards for the Portland Neighborhood System") are the operating rules — covering bylaws requirements, recognition criteria, open-meeting practices, and grievance procedures. The current Standards were drafted by the GREAT taskforce and adopted in 2005 (Ord. 179418, Res. 36329).

What changed in 2023

The definition of the Standards in City Code 3.96.020(F) was changed from "Regulations adopted by City Council" to a broader formulation, shifting the authority to revise the Standards from Council to the Civic Life office.

Where the process stands

Civic Life Director Amanda Garcia-Snell initially announced the rewrite would be enacted on a fixed timeline. The schedule has since shifted as public engagement and District Coalition feedback has continued.

Why this matters for FPNA

FPNA is one of 94 city-recognized neighborhood associations. Any change to the Standards affects how FPNA recognizes members, holds elections, conducts meetings, and engages with the city.

How to engage

Civic Life has invited public input through engagement sessions and draft review periods. The District Four Coalition leads the coordinated response across its member NAs. Residents wanting to weigh in can do so through D4C or directly with Civic Life.

Historical context: similar changes in 2019

Portland saw a comparable code-change effort under then-Commissioner Chloe Eudaly in 2019. That effort was reshaped after extended public engagement. The current process draws on that history.
