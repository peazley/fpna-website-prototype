Get Involved

Be part of the neighborhood association.

Membership is free. You'll receive occasional emails about meetings and neighborhood issues, invitations to general meetings, and a voice in the work the association does on behalf of residents.

Become a member

FPNA membership is open to residents, property owners, and business licensees within the FPNA boundary, free of charge. Per bylaws Article IV §1. Members receive:

  • Occasional email updates from FPNA about meetings and neighborhood issues
  • Invitations to general meetings and special events
  • Voting rights at the annual membership meeting
  • The ability to join committees and working groups
  • Direct line to the board on issues affecting residents

Not sure if you're in the boundary? Check your address on the about page.

Sign up

FPNA's official membership signup is hosted on AirTable through District Four Coalition. It takes about a minute.

Become a member →

You can also email president@forestparkneighbors.org to be added to the mailing list.

Volunteer

Ways to plug in.

FPNA's work is done by volunteer board members, committees, and ad-hoc working groups. The bylaws establish two standing committees; additional working groups form as needed.

Land Use Committee

Reviews land use notices and applications, drafts comment letters, and may file appeals on behalf of FPNA. One of two standing committees.

landuse@forestparkneighbors.org →

Communications Committee

Supports neighborhood engagement, event planning, the website, social media, and outreach. One of two standing committees.

board@forestparkneighbors.org →

50th Anniversary Planning (ad-hoc)

Coordinates neighborhood volunteer engagement, grant writing, partner presence, and local business outreach for the August 22 celebration.

board@forestparkneighbors.org →

Other neighborhood concerns are real and ongoing but are not necessarily formal FPNA committees — broadband access, Firewise, Neighborhood Emergency Teams (NETs), Forest Park stewardship, and PGE tree-trimming coordination are examples. Residents working in those areas are welcome to coordinate through the board.
