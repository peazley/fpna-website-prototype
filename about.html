About FPNA

A neighborhood association at the edge of one of America's largest urban forests.

FPNA is a volunteer-run, nonprofit neighborhood association representing residents who live in and around Portland's Forest Park — a broad area covering the neighborhoods, ridgelines, and hillsides that surround one of the largest urban forests in the country.

Mission

For fifty years, FPNA has worked to represent residents on the issues that shape our part of Portland — broadband access, wildfire preparedness, tree management and power reliability, land use, traffic, and environmental stewardship of one of the most ecologically significant urban landscapes in the country.

We hold monthly general meetings open to all residents, publish a regular Updates newsletter, and advocate on behalf of the neighborhood with the City of Portland, Multnomah County, and the State of Oregon. Membership is free and open to anyone living within the FPNA boundary.

What we do

  • Hold monthly general meetings open to all residents
  • Publish a regular Updates newsletter with neighborhood-relevant news
  • Advocate on behalf of residents on infrastructure, safety, and land use issues
  • Connect neighbors with one another and with adjacent partner organizations
  • Coordinate community responses to issues affecting multiple households
  • Steward the relationship between residents and Forest Park itself

What we are not

FPNA is not a homeowners' association. Membership is voluntary, there are no dues, no covenants, no design review, and no authority over private property. We are an organized voice for residents, not a regulatory body.

Boundary

Where the neighborhood begins and ends.

The FPNA boundary follows the residential edge of Forest Park itself, plus the surrounding ridge and valley communities. The exact boundary spans a mix of jurisdictions — many residents are in unincorporated Multnomah County, others are within the City of Portland — and it's erratic in places, a reflection of how this corner of the city actually developed. Fifty years

A short history of the association.

FPNA was organized in 1976 by a small group of residents concerned about the future of the neighborhood. Fifty years later, much has changed — but the work has stayed the same.

1976
Association founded
A group of residents organizes formally to represent the area in conversations with the City of Portland and Multnomah County.
1989
Forest Park boundaries expanded
FPNA advocates alongside other groups for the expansion of Forest Park itself, contributing to one of the most significant urban conservation efforts of the era.
2001
25th anniversary
The association marks 25 years with a community gathering and the first published history of the neighborhood.
2016
40th anniversary & partnership expansion
FPNA deepens its partnerships with Forest Park Conservancy, Skyline Ridge Neighbors, and the City of Portland Office of Community Technology.
2024
Broadband advocacy begins
Residents organize around documented gaps in federal broadband programs, surfacing the issue with state and federal officials.
2026
Fifty years
FPNA marks half a century of community organizing. Anniversary celebration on August 15, 2026.
